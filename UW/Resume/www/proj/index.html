Surface Ion Trap Electrostatic Simulation

Finite element simulations of electrostatics problems are relatively simple at a moderate distance from simple geometries. With expensive, professional tools, those limitations are also easily overcome with automatic divergence cancellations. Fortunately, the simple version is sufficient to simulate ion traps to a useful degree of accuracy. With some very simple finite element code I wrote myself, I've been able to simulate our trapping potentials to an accuracy of about 20 percent compared to measurements. The code has some general geometry creation routines and could be useful for any similar task as well.

Three.js Space Renderer

WebGL is a really cool technology, and support for it is growing quickly. I wanted to play around with visuallizing some simple problems with it, but ended up getting side tracked into writing an atmospheric scattering renderer. There are a number of tutorials out there that cover the basics, but the most physically realistic one here was written when graphics cards were significantly less powerful than today. In particular its possible to just run the entire simulation in GLSL on a GPU today.
